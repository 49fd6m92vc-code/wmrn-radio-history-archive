WMRN Broadcast Timeline

1940
December 23: WMRN-AM signs on the air in Marion, Ohio. Original frequency: 1500 kilocycles. First regular broadcast: 1:15 p.m.
1941
AM frequency changes to 1490 kilocycles under the North American Regional Broadcasting Agreement (NARBA).
1953
May 6: WMRN-FM signs on the air at 6:30 a.m.
Original FM frequency: 106.9 megacycles (Channel 295).
1960
December 23: 20th Anniversary Address reaffirms AM and FM dual-service operations.
2008
WMRN-FM frequency changes from 106.9 MHz to 95.1 MHz, reflecting updated FCC authorization and modernization of FM transmission facilities.
Present
WMRN-AM (1490 kHz) and successor FM operations are documented and preserved by the WMRN Radio History Archive.

Institutional Statement

The WMRN Radio History Archive exists to preserve, document, and provide structured access to the broadcast legacy of WMRN-AM and WMRN-FM in Marion, Ohio. Materials are cataloged, described, and presented in accordance with archival standards and historical documentation practices.
