# People & Programming

The history of WMRN is inseparable from the people who worked there and the programming they created. Beyond call letters and frequencies, the station was shaped day-to-day by voices, engineers, producers, reporters, and staff whose work connected with the Marion community in lasting ways.

This page exists to acknowledge those contributions and to begin documenting the individuals and programs that made WMRN what it was across different eras.

---

## On-Air & Programming Roles

Over the decades, WMRN featured a wide range of on-air programming, including news, music, sports, public affairs, and community features. The individuals behind these programs often served in multiple roles, sometimes simultaneously.

On-air and programming roles included, but were not limited to:

- Announcers and DJs  
- News directors and reporters  
- Sports broadcasters and commentators  
- Program directors and producers  
- Public affairs and community programming hosts  

Many of these roles evolved over time as formats, technology, and audience needs changed.

---

## Behind the Scenes

Equally important were the people whose work rarely made it to the microphone but was essential to keeping the station on the air.

Behind-the-scenes roles included:

- Engineers and technical staff  
- Operations and traffic personnel  
- Production and continuity staff  
- Office, administrative, and support staff  
- Sales and community relations teams  

Their contributions ensured that programming could happen reliably and professionally, day after day.

---

## Programming & Eras

Rather than attempting to catalog every program or airshift at once, this archive approaches programming history by era. Formats, schedules, and personnel changed over time, reflecting both broader industry trends and the needs of the local audience.

As the archive grows, this section will continue to expand to include:

- Program titles and formats  
- Approximate years or eras  
- Notable broadcasts or long-running programs  
- Community and public service programming  

This is an ongoing process, and completeness will improve as additional information becomes available.

---

## A Living Record

This page is not intended to be definitive or final. Radio history is often fragmented, and records are sometimes incomplete. Names may be missing, dates may be approximate, and memories may differ.

If you worked at WMRN, appeared on the air, supported programming, or have information that can help improve the accuracy or completeness of this section, contributions and corrections are welcome. Even small details — a program name, a role, or a rough timeframe — can help preserve an important piece of the station’s story.
